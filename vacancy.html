Community Minister

We currently have a vacancy for a Community Minister at WCF! If you are interested in applying please send your CV and a covering letter to recruit@wantagechristianfellowship.org.uk before the 25 Oct 2017.

Job Description – WCF Community Minister

The Role

To develop, through strategic planning and envisioning of the Fellowship, outreach initiatives to the community of Wantage and encourage a greater interest and involvement in overseas mission.

  • To provide pastoral support and care for those in the Fellowship and those in the community whose lives touch the Fellowship
  • To oversee and manage the integration, co-ordination and effectiveness of the Fellowship’s various ministries.
  • To actively engage and work with other ministers in the town and their congregations

Duties and Responsibilities

Using your entrepreneurial and pioneering skills:

  • Explore opportunities, initiate and be actively involved with the Fellowship’s outreach activities within the community of Wantage.
  • Identify, develop and coordinate both personal vision which already exists within the fellowship, and new ways of reaching the community.
  • Network with both local church and non-church groups within the town (i.e. schools, local charities, day centres, etc.)
  • Be the principal contact for the Fellowship for local, national and international links and networking, delegating to the Fellowship’s departments as appropriate.

With the eldership team:

Actively support and encourage the various ministries of WCF

  • Enable integration and co-ordination of each ministry within the overall programme of Fellowship activities to enhance and build the body of the Fellowship.
  • Work to develop gifting and leadership potential amongst WCF as a whole.
  • Live out and enable a culture of generous, relational Fellowship life which has a Christ-like character and enables spiritual growth within the Fellowship.
  • Take responsibility for Biblical teaching and preaching, delegating to and enabling others with suitable gifting as appropriate.
  • Take pastoral oversight and provide pastoral care to families, individuals and groups as necessary, working closely with the pastoral visiting team.
  • Facilitate the leading of discipleship groups, baptismal preparation classes and house groups
  • Provide pastoral care during life’s ‘rites of passage’ both within WCF and those whose lives touch the Fellowship – marriage preparation classes, presentation of infants, funerals and bereavement care.

Person Specification

It is essential that you are a Christian who can demonstrate the following:

  • that you hold an evangelical theology
  • that you accept the authority of Scripture and are able to deliver Biblical preaching and teaching to a high standard
  • that you have been baptised as a believer
  • that you have had proven fruitfulness in a previous Christian based ministry
  • that you are able to model a Spirit-filled, prayerful life, characterised by Bible study and devotional commitment
  • that you agree with and uphold our Statement of Faith
  • that you can effectively organise both yourself and others, showing a collaborative/inclusive leadership style
  • that you have effectively engaged with the other churches/ministers in your local community
  • that you have undertaken further theological studies and/or training

We are looking for a rich mix of many, but not necessarily all, of the following:

  • you show a mature, hospitable and welcoming approach
  • you are able to relate well to all age groups
  • you are able to build bridges into community organisations
  • you can operate effectively without a specific church building
  • you have experienced work routine outside of the Christian ministry
  • you have a good working knowledge of key IT applications including MS Office suite or equivalent and major social media platforms
